# Basecamp Rewards – Customer Feedback Synthesis
**20 documents analyzed** · Surveys (6) · Emails (6) · Interviews (4) · Support Tickets (4)
*Synthesized: November 2024*

---

## Executive Summary

Customers broadly like the *concept* of Basecamp Rewards but are being driven away by a broken app and a points expiration policy that's never communicated upfront. These two issues dominate the feedback and are actively causing churn among previously loyal customers. Staff quality is highly inconsistent across locations — Pearl District excels, Alberta is a recurring problem. A strong undercurrent of demand exists for a VIP tier system and expanded redemption options, particularly seasonal drinks. The program has real goodwill to build on, but technical and communication failures are eroding it fast.

---

## Key Themes

### 1. App Reliability Is the Single Biggest Problem
*Sources: email_complaint_app, email_mixed_feedback, interview_regular_customer, interview_former_regular, interview_new_customer, interview_skeptical_customer, survey_001, survey_002, survey_004, survey_005, ticket_001, ticket_002, email_suggestion_tiers*

The app is mentioned critically in **13 of 20 documents** — by far the most dominant topic. Problems reported include:

- **Crashes** — Jennifer Martinez (BC-6621): "The app crashed. Restarted — now shows 0 points." Lisa M. describes opening the QR code before getting in line just in case it crashes during checkout.
- **Wrong balance display** — Survey BC-4892 saw their balance flip from 340 → 280 → 340 in one session. Jennifer Martinez's balance reset to zero.
- **Login failures** — Mark D. (TKT-2024-1892) couldn't log in for 3 days across two devices. Support notes flag a "possible pattern" with 3 similar tickets already open.
- **Sync delays** — Points routinely take hours to appear after purchase (Tyler R. interview, Nicole F. ticket, survey BC-6206). Multiple customers express anxiety about whether their points are actually safe.

Only one customer (survey BC-3156) reports a flawless app experience — and even the overall program's highest-rated respondent notes "sometimes it takes a while."

**Contradiction:** Tyler R. (software developer) calls the app "perfectly adequate for a coffee shop." James W. (attorney, non-member) watched multiple people fumbling with it in line and cited app problems as reinforcement of his decision not to join.

### 2. Points Expiration Policy — Good Policy, Catastrophic Communication
*Sources: email_complaint_points, interview_former_regular, interview_regular_customer, email_mixed_feedback, survey_002, survey_004, survey_006, email_praise_staff, interview_new_customer*

The 60-day expiration policy appears **fair in principle but is almost never explained at sign-up**, causing repeated trust-breaking moments:

- Robert Kowalski (BC-3847) lost 142 saved points after a 2-month overseas trip. He was never told. Threatening to leave reviews.
- Patricia K. (interview_former_regular) *stopped coming entirely* after losing points twice — once because her card was never "activated," once from travel. "It felt like a trap."
- Survey BC-7231 lost 89 points. "That's not a loyalty program, that's a points trap."

**The fix is clear:** customers who *were* told upfront reacted completely differently.
- Michelle Nguyen praised Kenji (Pearl District) specifically for explaining expiration on a napkin.
- Tyler R. (new customer) said he'd "probably have been annoyed to find that out the hard way" — Devon told him at sign-up and he was grateful.
- Survey BC-6206: Devon explained it, customer found the policy "fair."

**Contradiction:** Daily regulars (Lisa M., survey BC-6206) think 60 days is perfectly reasonable. Travelers and occasional visitors (Robert K., Patricia K., survey BC-7231) experience it as a punishment. The policy may need differentiation — or at minimum a warning email before expiration.

- **Priya** (Division) — called "a rewards program encyclopedia" by Lisa M.

### 4. Redemption Options Are Too Limited
*Sources: email_praise_rewards, email_suggestion_tiers, interview_regular_customer, survey_001, survey_003, survey_005, survey_006, email_mixed_feedback*

Requested additions, in rough order of frequency:
1. **Seasonal / specialty drinks** — Lisa M., survey BC-3156, David O'Connor, survey BC-5543
2. **Merchandise** (tumblers, etc.) — Tom Bradley, survey BC-3156, Lisa M.
3. **Vegetarian / dietary options** — survey BC-5543: "Half the food redemptions I can't eat."
4. **Local bakery partnerships** — survey BC-2209, survey BC-4543
5. **Exclusive member events / tastings** — survey BC-3156

Tom Bradley noted he'd already referred two friends specifically because of the program. More redemption variety would likely amplify word-of-mouth.

### 5. High Demand for a VIP Tier System
*Sources: email_suggestion_tiers, interview_regular_customer, survey_005, email_mixed_feedback*

Multiple customers surfaced this independently, suggesting unmet demand:

Amanda Chen (BC-5178) submitted the most detailed proposal — Silver/Gold/Platinum tiers with bonus point multipliers, surprise rewards, birthday drinks, and exclusive tastings. She's spending $400+ at the shop since launch and gets identical benefits to a once-a-week visitor.

Lisa M. (daily customer since August, ~600 points earned): "Would be nice to be recognized somehow. Not asking for a parade, but maybe a 'gold member' badge."

Survey BC-5543: "I come here every single day and get the same rewards as someone who comes once a week. Would be nice to have VIP status."

---

### 6. Value Perception Divides Along Visitor Frequency
*Sources: email_praise_rewards, interview_new_customer, survey_003, survey_002, interview_skeptical_customer*

**Contradiction:** Frequent visitors (daily+) tend to perceive the earning rate as generous — Tom Bradley and survey BC-3156 both say they get a free drink every couple of weeks. Infrequent visitors and the non-member skeptic (James W.) calculate a 3.3% return and find it uncompelling compared to "every 10th drink free" formats.

James W. proposed an alternative model entirely: a flat membership discount (e.g., 10% off always, paid upfront) with no tracking. This isn't currently offered and represents a segment that the points model may never convert.

## Patterns by Customer Segment

**Daily regulars** (Lisa M., Amanda Chen, survey BC-3156, survey BC-5543): Generally positive, want tier recognition, seasonal drink redemptions, and a better app. Low churn risk unless app problems worsen.

**Occasional visitors / travelers** (Robert K., Patricia K., survey BC-7231): Highest churn risk. Expiration policy hits them disproportionately. Patricia K. already left for Stumptown.

**New customers** (Tyler R., Tom Bradley): Positive first impressions. Onboarding quality determines long-term experience — Tyler got a good sign-up, Patricia didn't.

**Older / less tech-savvy customers** (Michelle Nguyen, George T.): Prefer physical cards, need more onboarding support. Can be won over with patience (Kenji's napkin story). Not currently at risk of leaving but need accommodation.

**Privacy-conscious non-joiners** (James W.): Unlikely to convert on current model. Might respond to a flat discount membership alternative.

## Recommended Actions

**Immediate (low cost, high impact):**
1. **Add expiration warning emails** — 14-day and 3-day notices before points expire. This alone would have prevented Robert K., Patricia K., and survey BC-7231 from feeling betrayed.
2. **Add expiration info to sign-up flow** — Make it prominent, not fine print. Kenji's napkin is the gold standard; replicate it systematically.
3. **Retrain Alberta location** — Three support tickets this month on redemption failures. A single training session would fix this.

**Short-term (1-3 months):**
4. **Fix app login and sync bugs** — TKT-2024-1892 and its two flagged similar tickets suggest an account-level authentication bug. The sync delay issue is damaging trust even among otherwise happy customers.
5. **Add seasonal drinks to redemption menu** — Mentioned by multiple regulars independently. Low friction, high emotional payoff ("the autumn maple latte is my weakness").
6. **Standardize sign-up onboarding across all locations** — Inconsistent knowledge is the root cause of most expiration complaints. A laminated "what to tell every new member" card would help.

**Medium-term (3-6 months):**
7. **Develop VIP tier system** — Multiple customers surfaced this unprompted. Amanda Chen's proposal is a workable starting point. The psychological pull of "status" will increase visit frequency and average spend.
8. **Expand food redemptions** — Add vegetarian options and seasonal items. Consider a local bakery pilot at one location.
9. **Add push notifications** — When points are expiring, and when a customer has enough to redeem. Tyler R.'s suggestion: "I'm not going to remember to check."

## Questions That Remain Unanswered

- How widespread is the app login bug (TKT-2024-1892)? Agent notes say "possible pattern" with 3 similar tickets — total affected users unknown.
- What is the actual churn rate from expiration events? Patricia K. is confirmed gone; how many others left silently?
- Are non-app users (physical card only, like George T.) tracking differently from app users? They have no visibility into their balance.
- What percentage of signups are receiving the clear upfront explanation that Devon and Kenji give? This seems to be the exception, not the rule.
- Is the Alberta location the only undertrained site, or are there others?
- Has anyone modeled whether a tier system would increase average spend enough to cover the cost?
